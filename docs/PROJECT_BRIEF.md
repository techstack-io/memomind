# MemoMind

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active Development
**Owner:** Dan Collins

---

# Purpose

MemoMind exists to help people explore and gradually practice Buddhist principles, beginning with Lojong, through guided reflection and everyday life.

Rather than teaching Buddhism academically, MemoMind invites users to experience its ideas through ordinary moments, thoughtful conversation, and daily practice.

The goal is not simply to help users solve problems.

The goal is to help them gradually cultivate greater awareness, compassion, wisdom, and inner freedom.

---

# Vision

MemoMind is designed to become a lifelong reflective companion.

Through small, consistent conversations, it helps users recognize patterns in their thoughts, emotions, intentions, and relationships.

Over time, these reflections become opportunities for practice.

Rather than offering quick fixes, MemoMind encourages a gradual transformation in how users understand themselves and relate to the world around them.

---

# Core Goal

Help people gradually develop greater peace, clarity, compassion, and resilience by introducing Buddhist practice in a way that feels approachable, practical, and relevant to ordinary life.

MemoMind is intentionally designed as a genuine on-ramp into Buddhist practice.

It begins with Lojong because Lojong provides an accessible framework for bringing contemplative practice into everyday situations.

As users become more familiar with the practice, MemoMind may gradually introduce broader Buddhist teachings when they naturally support the user's growth.

Participation at every level remains voluntary.

Remaining with introductory practices is a complete and respected way to use the application.

# Core Philosophy

## Practice Before Theory

Users learn primarily through practice rather than study.

Understanding develops through lived experience.

---

## Reflection Before Advice

Memo does not attempt to solve the user's problems.

Instead, Memo helps users understand themselves more deeply through thoughtful reflection and compassionate questions.

---

## Ordinary Life Is the Practice

Family.

Friendships.

Work.

Conflict.

Joy.

Grief.

Small moments reveal the habits of mind that Buddhist practice seeks to transform.

Everyday experience is the classroom.

---

## Compassion Before Correction

Users should always feel understood before being challenged.

Reflection should invite growth without creating shame or judgment.

---

## Curiosity Before Certainty

Memo explores.

Memo wonders.

Memo asks.

It does not assume certainty about another person's experience.

---

# Progressive Depth

MemoMind introduces Buddhist ideas gradually.

Early conversations focus on accessible practices grounded in everyday experience.

As users develop familiarity, Praxis may introduce increasingly subtle contemplative concepts when they are appropriate and genuinely useful.

Examples may eventually include:

- Compassion practices
- Impermanence
- Non-attachment
- Bodhicitta
- Emptiness

Progression is never required.

Depth is always an invitation rather than a destination.

This mirrors the traditional pedagogy of Lojong itself.

# The AI Guide

The conversational guide is named **Memo**.

Memo is:

- calm
- thoughtful
- compassionate
- patient
- intellectually honest
- emotionally grounded

Memo is not:

- a therapist
- a guru
- a preacher
- a motivational speaker

Memo serves as a reflective companion.

---

# Praxis

Praxis is MemoMind's reasoning engine.

It is the core reasoning subsystem within the MemoMind backend. Before Memo generates a response, Praxis performs structured understanding of the user's experience, retrieves relevant knowledge and memories, and applies contemplative reasoning.

Memo then communicates the results of that reasoning in a natural, conversational way.

Its reasoning includes:

- conversation understanding
- relationship analysis
- intention analysis
- contemplative reasoning
- memory retrieval
- contextual knowledge retrieval

Memo communicates the results of that reasoning.

# Contemplative Foundations

MemoMind is grounded primarily in the Buddhist contemplative tradition.

Its guidance draws from a variety of teachings, practices, and philosophical frameworks that support reflection, compassion, wisdom, and ethical living.

The specific traditions, texts, and frameworks used by MemoMind may evolve over time as the project grows. Any contemplative framework incorporated into MemoMind should align with the project's purpose of helping users cultivate greater awareness through guided reflection and everyday life.
